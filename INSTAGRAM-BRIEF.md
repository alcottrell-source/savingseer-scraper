## 1. What the product is

**Tide** (tidego.co) tells UK shoppers **when it's worth going to a shopping centre** —
i.e. when a meaningful share of the shops there are on sale at the same time. Think
"surf report, but for mall sales." It tracks ~90 high-street brands across ~37 UK
shopping centres and, each day, publishes a per-centre **Tide Score** and a plain-English
verdict. Sales are **human-verified** (an operator confirms each brand's sale), so the
data is trustworthy, not scraped hype.

The core promise, in the brand's own words: **"See when it's worth the trip."**

## 2. The concepts that drive every post

- **Tide Score** — a single % = the share of tracked shops at a centre currently on sale.
  Always shown alongside the raw count, e.g. **"58% · 23 of 40 shops on sale."** The number
  is checkable, never vague.
- **Verdict words** (the only vocabulary we use):
  | Verdict | Meaning | Use in copy |
  |---|---|---|
  | **PEAK / GO NOW** | Sale density just crested — best day to go | The urgency post |
  | **RISING** | Building toward a peak | "Worth watching" |
  | **EASING** | Past the peak, winding down | "Last chance" energy, gently |
  | **QUIET** | Little on | Rarely posted |
  | **OVER** | Sale window done | Not posted |
- **Per-shop facts** — for any centre we know, for each shop on sale: **how new the sale is**
  ("started today / 3 days ago") and **how deep** ("up to 60% off").
- **Two natural post angles**: *(a)* by **centre** ("Westquay just hit peak — here's what's on")
  and *(b)* by **brand** ("River Island is 60% off at 22 centres right now").

## 3. Brand identity (keep posts on-brand)

- **Tone:** calm, factual, quietly confident. We describe what's happening; we never
  scream "MEGA SALE!!!". The *only* place we tell people to act is the **PEAK / "go now"**
  moment. Everything else is direction, not a command.
- **Palette:** near-black `#0B0B0D`, cream `#F5F1EB`, signal green `#5EFFB0` (the hero
  number / CTA), warm amber `#FFD79A` (PEAK + discount %).
- **Type:** Playfair Display (serif, for big emotional headlines + the TIDE wordmark) and
  Inter (everything functional, including all numbers).
- **Visual signature:** dark backdrop, one big number, the verdict word, a short ranked
  list. See the attached reel cover — that's the look.

## 4. My situation & constraints (important — design the strategy around these)

- **Solo founder, early stage.** Limited time; no budget for paid ads yet. Organic only.
- **No face on camera.** I will *not* appear in reels. Content must work as **static,
  text-and-data visuals** (a designed card / a centre photo + the sales), lightly animated —
  not talking-head or lifestyle footage.
- **I can produce:** branded image/video cards from my own live data (I already have a
  generator and a reel-cover template — see §5). I can shoot the occasional photo of a
  local centre on my phone, but assume **most posts have no original photography**.
- **Audience:** UK deal-conscious shoppers — students, parents, bargain-hunters, people
  planning a shopping trip. Hyperlocal angle matters (each centre = a local community).
- **Distribution etiquette:** on borrowed platforms (Reddit, local Facebook groups) I must
  give value first and not spam links. Instagram is *my* platform, so I can post freely there.

## 5. Assets I already have

1. **In-app share card** — tapping "Share" on tidego.co generates a branded square image of
   a centre's current tide (score, verdict, GO NOW, biggest discounts) for users to repost.
2. **Admin "sales overview"** — an internal dashboard listing every centre by number of sales
   plus, per centre, the **newest** and **biggest-discount** shops. This is my daily source
   for *what to post about*.
3. **Reel/Story cover template** (attached image) — a 9:16 vertical frame: hook headline over
   a hero backdrop (brand gradient, or a real centre photo if I have one), then PEAK badge,
   the big % score, "N of M shops on sale", a ranked "Biggest discounts today" list, and a
   "Full list → tidego.co" call-to-action. It's parameterised — I change a few fields per post.

## 6. What I want from you (the new chat)

Build an Instagram growth plan for a **no-face, data-driven, static/animated** account, specifically:

1. **Content pillars** — 3–5 repeatable post types I can run forever off my data
   (e.g. "Peak alert", "Centre leaderboard", "Brand deep-discount", "Sale calendar").
2. **A 2-week starter calendar** — what to post each day, which pillar, which format
   (reel vs carousel vs story), and the exact on-screen text + caption + hook for each.
3. **Reel scripts without a face** — for ~6 reels: the 3-second hook, the on-screen text
   beats, suggested motion (e.g. Ken Burns zoom, number count-up, list pop-ins), length,
   and what kind of trending audio to look for. Assume I'll assemble them in CapCut or Canva.
4. **Hooks & captions** — a swipe file of 20+ scroll-stopping opening lines in my brand voice
   (calm/factual, urgency only on PEAK), plus a caption formula and CTA patterns.
5. **Hashtag + discovery strategy** — UK shopping/deals tags, local-centre tags, and how to
   use location tags; how to seed early reach when follower count is ~0.
6. **Cadence & growth loop** — realistic posting frequency for a solo founder, how to turn
   the in-app share card into user-generated reach, and 2–3 cheap experiments to find what
   sticks before scaling.

Keep advice concrete and matched to a pre-launch solo founder. Prioritise things that are
**free, repeatable from my own data, and don't require me on camera.**
